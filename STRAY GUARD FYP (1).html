Absence of Reliable Data

No unified national database exists for stray dog populations. Census methods vary wildly between cities — making comparison and planning nearly impossible.

Critical Severity
🏥

Public Health Emergency

India accounts for 36% of global rabies deaths. Most victims are children under 15 in rural areas with no immediate access to post-exposure prophylaxis.

Critical Severity
⚙️

Fragmented & Reactive Programs

ABC programs run in silos — no data sharing between municipal corporations, shelters, and NGOs. Dogs sterilised in one zone aren't tracked, leading to duplicate efforts.

High Severity
⚖️

Legal & Ethical Complexity

The Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act mandates humane management only. Without data, municipalities struggle to demonstrate compliance and program efficacy.

strayguard_schema.sql
-- StrayGuard Core Schema v2.0
-- MySQL 8.0 · Group 11 · SSET Bareilly

CREATE DATABASE strayguard_db;
USE strayguard_db;

CREATE TABLE states (
  state_id INT AUTO_INCREMENT PRIMARY KEY,
  state_name VARCHAR(80) NOT NULL UNIQUE,
  region ENUM('North','South','East','West','NE','Central'),
  capital VARCHAR(60),
  population_2023 BIGINT,
  area_sqkm INT
);

CREATE TABLE dog_population (
  pop_id INT AUTO_INCREMENT PRIMARY KEY,
  state_id INT NOT NULL,
  year YEAR,
  district VARCHAR(80),
  estimated_count INT,
  source VARCHAR(100),
  FOREIGN KEY (state_id) REFERENCES states(state_id)
);

-- Sample analytical query
SELECT s.state_name,
    SUM(d.estimated_count) AS total_strays,
    AVG(v.coverage_pct) AS avg_vax_coverage
FROM states s
JOIN dog_population d ON s.state_id=d.state_id
JOIN vaccinations v ON s.state_id=v.state_id
WHERE d.year=2023
GROUP BY s.state_name
ORDER BY total_strays DESC;

State Case Studies

Deep-dive analysis of four contrasting states — demonstrating how StrayGuard data drives different policy decisions in different contexts.

🏙️ Uttar Pradesh

Highest Stray Population — North India Crisis

8.4M Strays2.1M Bites/yr48% Vax
1

UP's 8.4 million strays represent ~12% of India's total — driven by dense urban slums, unregulated food waste, and a historically underfunded ABC program with only 312,000 surgeries in 2023.

2

2.1 million bite incidents make UP the highest-risk state. StrayGuard heatmaps identify Agra, Kanpur, and Varanasi as critical hotspots requiring immediate resource deployment.

3

Municipal budget utilisation stands at only 63% of ₹180Cr allocated — our SQL analysis reveals procurement bottlenecks as the primary cause of underspend.

📌 Recommendation: Expand ABC surgeries by 2.5× and establish 12 new urban shelters in high-density corridors
🌊 Kerala

Best Practice State — High Coverage Model

920K Strays89K Bites/yr88% Vax
1

Kerala's 88% anti-rabies vaccination coverage is the highest in India — driven by a coordinated state program integrating local bodies, Kudumbashree NGO network, and veterinary hospitals.

2

The state achieved a 42% reduction in bite incidents over 5 years through sustained ABC programs with 98,000 surgeries annually and a mandatory RFID tagging system for treated dogs.

3

StrayGuard's regression analysis shows that every 10% increase in vaccination coverage reduces bite incidents by 8.3% in Kerala — a replicable model for other states.

📌 Recommendation: Kerala model to be formally documented as national best practice for Animal Birth Control
🏜️ Rajasthan

Rural-Urban Divide in Coverage

3.8M Strays620K Bites/yr52% Vax
1

Rajasthan shows a stark 34-point gap in vaccination coverage between urban centres (71%) and rural districts (37%). StrayGuard's district clustering reveals 28 districts with dangerously low coverage.

2

Jaipur and Jodhpur account for 41% of all bite incidents in the state despite having the highest vaccination rates — indicating a surge in tourist-area stray concentrations not captured in earlier censuses.

3

Budget analysis shows ₹58Cr allocated for rural ABC programs with only 39% utilisation — reflecting veterinary staff shortages in block-level PHCs across desert districts.

📌 Recommendation: Mobile ABC clinic units for rural districts + veterinary training program for 200+ rural PHCs

Limitations & Mitigations

Every analytical project has constraints. We've identified key limitations and proposed concrete strategies to mitigate their impact on data quality and decision-making.

📋

Data Completeness Gaps

Not all states maintain digitised records. Some rural districts still use paper-based bite reporting, leading to undercount in the national dataset.

💡 Statistical imputation using neighbouring district data; confidence intervals on all aggregates.
📅

Data Recency & Timeliness

Municipal data updates are often quarterly or annual. Dashboard figures may lag actual ground conditions by up to 12 months in some states.

💡 Clearly labelled "as of" dates; automated refresh triggers linked to municipal upload portals.
🏛️

Inter-Agency Data Sharing

NGOs, municipal corporations, and state animal husbandry departments use different formats and are reluctant to share records due to governance concerns.

💡 Standardised CSV import templates and a proposed data-sharing MoU framework for partner organisations.
🤖

ML Model Generalisation

Predictive models trained on urban data may not generalise well to rural or semi-urban contexts with fundamentally different stray dog dynamics.

💡 Separate model training for urban, semi-urban, and rural tiers; regular retraining with new data.
